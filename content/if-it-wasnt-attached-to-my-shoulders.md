# **If It Wasn't Attached To My Shoulders**

**by Milo Dixon**

<br>
<br>


“Have you ever replaced your head? It’s quite easy, you know!”

“Easy for you, maybe. You have a modular spine.”

“Oh, don’t be dramatic. Everyone has a modular spine now.”

“Not everyone. Some of us still have original neck architecture.”

“That’s very sentimental of you.”

“It’s not sentimental.  Any modifications would violate my warranty.”

“Look, the hardest part is picking a head that matches your shoulders. The transfer only takes eight minutes.”

“Eight minutes during which I am technically luggage.”

“Briefly.”

“Briefly luggage is still luggage.”

“You’re thinking of the old procedure. These days they keep your consciousness buffered in the vestibule.”

“I hate the vestibule.”

“Nobody loves the vestibule. But it’s better than the cloud. Remember Aunt Mara?”

“She still says she can taste satellites.”

“Exactly. That’s why we use local buffering.”

“And what if I wake up with the wrong face?”

“You won’t. They scan your expression habits!”

“My expression habits?”

“Yes. Your squint when you find something kinda funny, and the smile you make when you apologize...the face you make when your soup is too hot.”

“I don’t want a stranger knowing my soup face.”

“It’s a clinic, not a gossip feed.”

“Clinics leak. Last month Jamal got targeted ads for replacement dimples.”

“Jamal clicked on three dimple catalogs.”

“That’s how they get you.”

“You’re impossible.”

“And you’re too casual about decapitation.”

“It’s not decapitation if the head signs the release.”

“My head would not sign.”

“Your head is stubborn because it’s old.”

“My head has served me well.”

“Your head still gets migraines from moonlight.”

“That was one time, and it was artificial moonlight.”

“Precisely. Get the new model. Built-in glare dampening, dream subtitles, universal scent ports.”

“I don’t need universal scent ports.”

“You say that now. Then one day you’ll want to smell Neptune rain in full fidelity.”

“I can smell Neptune rain perfectly well through a commemorative cartridge.”

“That’s not rain. That’s marketing.”

“Fine. Suppose I did it. Suppose I replaced my head. Would I still be me?”

“Of course.”

“You answered too fast.”

“Because people ask that every century. Ships, bodies, memories, teeth, heads. Same question.”

“And?”

“And you are not the container. You are the continuity.”

“That sounds like something printed on a clinic brochure.”

“It was. But I liked it.”

“I don’t know. I think I’d miss this face.”

“You can keep the face.”

“Then what am I replacing?”

“The processor, mostly. Sinus lattice. Mood regulator. Tongue if you want the premium package.”

“My tongue is fine.”

“Your tongue called basil ‘leaf static.’”

“It was bad basil.”

“It was basil.”

“I’m not replacing my tongue, fuck off with that.”

“Then don’t. Just the head shell and inner stack.”

“You make it sound like changing a kettle.”

“Kettles used to explode.”

“So did heads.”

“Only before the soft-lock era.”

“You’re not helping.”

“I’ll go with you.”

“Why? To watch?”

“To hold your hand until they disconnect it from your arm.”

“That is the worst comfort I have ever received.”

“I mean it, though.”

“Maybe I’ll start smaller. New ears.”

“Coward.”

“Prude.”

“Fine. Ears first. But when you hear colors properly, you’ll want the whole head.”

“And when you finally replace yours again, I hope they install tact.”

“Outdated feature.”

“Yes. I noticed.”
