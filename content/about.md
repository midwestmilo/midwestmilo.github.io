I’m Milo Dixon, a Chicago-based writer and poet.

I write because people are often softer, funnier, and more complicated than they are allowed to be.

C-Minus is where I keep that attention: fiction, poems, experiments, code-shaped pieces, field notes, and small machines built out of feeling. The name comes from the grade I often received in Conduct on my elementary school report card.

My work is personal, but it is not separate from the professional and technical worlds I have lived in. These are places where people get sorted into policies, workflows, tickets, metrics, reports, performance gaps, and “best practices.”

My technical interests come from the same place as my writing: I want to understand how things are built, why they fail, and what they reveal when they do.

My debut poetry collection, *Specimens*, was published in 2026.

Between my poems, short stories, and the evolution of *STDERR*, I am trying to make work that feels emotionally honest and structurally alive.
