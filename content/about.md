# About

C-Minus is the personal writing site of Milo Dixon: a home for fiction, poems, notes, projects, and small systems that are still becoming what they are.

The work here moves through speculative fiction, grief, faith, queerness, climate anxiety, class, odd bureaucracy, body questions, friendship, machines, memory, and the strange ways people try to stay human inside systems that keep asking for proof.

Some pieces are polished. Some are experiments. Some are stories pretending to be diagrams, poems pretending to be field notes, or jokes that accidentally found a wound. The common thread is attention: to language, to pressure, to the body, to the weather, to the machinery around us, and to the small tendernesses that survive anyway.

Milo Dixon is a Chicago-based writer and poet. His debut poetry collection, *Specimens*, was published by C-Minus in 2026. His work often lives somewhere between confession and apparatus: emotionally direct, structurally curious, and interested in what breaks, what persists, and what still refuses to be pinned down.
